---
title: "Auto-unstuck: how Passivbot recovers stuck positions"
description: "How Passivbot's auto-unstuck mechanism realizes small losses to recover from stuck positions, with configuration parameters and operational notes."
---

A position is "stuck" when price has moved far enough against it that the wallet exposure limit has been reached and no further re-entries are possible. Without intervention, a stuck position sits at a high average entry price while funding fees erode the balance. Auto-unstuck breaks this cycle by performing controlled partial closes at a small loss, then allowing normal re-entries at lower prices, gradually bringing the average entry price closer to the market. Rather than a single large stop-loss, it chips away at the position over time.

## How it works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Detect stuck position">
    The bot compares each position's current wallet exposure against its limit. When the ratio exceeds `unstuck_threshold`, the position is considered stuck and unstucking is activated for it.

    ```text
    if wallet_exposure / wallet_exposure_limit > unstuck_threshold:
        unstucking enabled
    ```

    If multiple positions are stuck simultaneously, the bot selects the one with the **smallest gap between average entry price and current market price** — that is, the least underwater position — to unstuck first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Place a partial close at a loss">
    A small reduce-only order is placed near the upper EMA band (for long) or lower EMA band (for short), deliberately accepting a loss to reduce the position size.

    ```text
    long_unstuck_close_price  = upper_EMA_band * (1 + unstuck_ema_dist)
    short_unstuck_close_price = lower_EMA_band * (1 - unstuck_ema_dist)
    ```

    The close quantity is:

    ```text
    unstuck_close_qty = full_pos_size * wallet_exposure_limit * unstuck_close_pct
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Re-enter at a lower price">
    After the partial close reduces the position and frees up margin, the bot places new re-entry orders using the normal grid or trailing logic. These entries are at a lower (long) or higher (short) price than the previous average, reducing the average entry price.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Repeat until recovered">
    The cycle continues — partial close, re-entry, partial close — until the average entry price is close enough to the market that normal take-profit closes can exit the position at a profit. The process is capped by `unstuck_loss_allowance_pct` to prevent runaway drawdown.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Configuration parameters

| Parameter | Description |
|---|---|
| `unstuck_close_pct` | Fraction of `full_pos_size × wallet_exposure_limit` to close with each unstuck order |
| `unstuck_ema_dist` | Distance from the EMA band to place the unstuck close order. Negative values move the price closer to the band; positive moves it further away |
| `unstuck_loss_allowance_pct` | Weighted percentage below past peak balance to allow losses before pausing unstucking |
| `unstuck_threshold` | Fraction of `wallet_exposure_limit` at which a position is considered stuck |

**Loss allowance formula:**

```text
loss_allowance = past_peak_balance * (1 - unstuck_loss_allowance_pct * total_wallet_exposure_limit)
```

**Example:** past peak balance `$10,000`, `unstuck_loss_allowance_pct = 0.02`, `total_wallet_exposure_limit = 1.5`:

```text
loss_allowance = $10,000 * (1 - 0.02 * 1.5) = $9,700
```

The bot stops taking unstuck losses once balance reaches $9,700. It resumes only after other positions generate enough profit to lift the balance floor.

**Unstuck threshold example:** if a position has WE of `0.50` and `wallet_exposure_limit = 1.0`, the position is 50% full. With `unstuck_threshold = 0.45`, unstucking activates and continues until the position is reduced to 45% of the limit.

## Multi-coin advantage

Auto-unstuck is significantly more effective when running across multiple coins simultaneously.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Prioritization" icon="arrow-up-1-9">
    The bot selects the least underwater position to unstuck first, allowing rapid recovery of the most recoverable coin before moving to deeper bags.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Profit offsetting" icon="scale-balanced">
    While one coin realizes small losses to unstuck, other active coins continue generating take-profit income. The equity curve stays smoother than with a single-coin setup.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Capped drawdown" icon="gauge-max">
    Total unstuck losses are bounded by `unstuck_loss_allowance_pct`. After the allowance is consumed, further losses are only permitted after other positions have recovered the balance floor.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Weakness: black-swan events

<Warning>
Auto-unstuck is not a defense against extreme, prolonged unilateral price moves. In black-swan scenarios — exchange failures, stablecoin depegs, delistings, or sustained one-directional crashes — the mechanism may continue taking losses and re-entering into an adversely moving coin. The loss allowance will eventually pause it, but damage can still accumulate before the floor is hit. **Human intervention is the final backstop** for these edge cases.
</Warning>

## Interaction with other risk systems

Auto-unstuck operates alongside the other risk layers. Understanding how they interact prevents unexpected behavior.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Equity hard stop loss (HSL)">
    If HSL triggers RED on a `pside`, that side is panic-closed and halted. Auto-unstuck cannot operate on a halted `pside`. After the HSL cooldown and restart, auto-unstuck resumes from the current state as if starting fresh.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="WEL enforcer">
    The WEL enforcer (`risk_wel_enforcer_threshold`) may also issue reduce-only orders on an oversized position. Both the enforcer and auto-unstuck can act on the same position. The enforcer targets positions above the effective WE cap; auto-unstuck targets positions above `unstuck_threshold` of the limit.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Realized-loss gate">
    `max_realized_loss_pct` applies to all loss-realizing closes, including auto-unstuck orders. If the projected balance after an unstuck close would fall below the configured floor, the order is blocked. Only panic closes are exempt from this gate.

    If the realized-loss gate is set too tightly, it can prevent auto-unstuck from operating entirely. Use panic mode for forced reduction when the gate is blocking necessary closes.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
